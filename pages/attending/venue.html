The Current Situation of Kasai International Airport (KIX)

(Updated Sep.12, 2018)
Kansai International Airport (KIX), the nearest international airport to Awaji, was seriously damaged on September 4, 2018 due to the typhoon Maymay. It is now in recovery with strong support of Japanese Government. The current status and the anticipated schedule are as follows;

  • Kansai International Airport has two terminals (Terminal 1 for FSCs and some LCCs, and Terminal 2 for some LCCs) and two Runways (A and B).
  • Terminal 2 and Runway B are already in operation.
  • Runway 1 and some part of Terminal 1 are expected in operation in this week.
  • The bridge between the airport and the mainland was damaged. The road is partially open now. The railways are expected to resume by the end of September. Currently bus connection is provided.
  • Although the airport is regaining the functionality step by step, flight recovery is up to individual airlines. Please contact your airlines if needed.
  • Some flights including international flights are planned to change their destinations from Kansai Airport to Osaka International (Itami)/ Kobe Airports.
The alternative is to use the airports in Tokyo (Narita and Haneda Airports) or Nagoya (Chubu International Airport). You can reach Kansai area by flights to Osaka International (Itami) Airport and Kobe Airports or the fast trains (Shinkansen) from there.
  • By flights
    - From Haneda/Narita Airports to Osaka International (Itami) Airport : 1.5 hours
    - From Haneda/Narita Airports to Kobe Airport : 1.5 hours
  • By the fast trains (Shinkansen)
    - From Haneda Airport to Sannnomiya station : 3 hours
    - From Narita Airport to Sannnomiya station : 4 hours
    - From Chubu International Airport to Sannnomiya station : 2.5 hours
All other infrastructures in Kansai area are not damaged. You can come to Awaji as usual.

About

The Awaji Yumebutai International Conference Center is a resort and conference center surrounded by a rich natural environment. The conference center assures you of a success in every conceivable type of international and academic conference, as well as incentives, trainings, seminars, or ceremonies.

Access

Awaji Yumebutai International Conference Center is located at Awaji Island, Hogyo prefecture, Japan. Hyogo is located at west area of Japan (450km from Tokyo). The nearest international airport is Kansai International Airport (KIX) which is about 120 minutes from/to the venue by train and bus. There are several ways to get to the venue. Recommended ways are as follows. For other ways, please refer to this page.
